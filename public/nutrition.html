The Digestive System Assembly Line

Click the "Start" or "Next Step" button to see how food travels through your digestive system and gets broken down.

Ready to Eat!

Imagine a piece of food, like a chapati. Let's see what happens to it. Click "Start" to begin the journey.

1. The Mouth 👄

Mechanical Digestion: Your teeth (✂️) cut, tear, and grind the food into small pieces. This is called mastication.
Chemical Digestion: Saliva mixes with the food. An enzyme called salivary amylase starts breaking down complex carbohydrates (starch) into simpler sugars.

2. The Oesophagus (Food Pipe)

The tongue pushes the chewed food, now called a 'bolus', to the back of the throat. It then travels down the oesophagus through a wave-like muscular movement called peristalsis. It's like squeezing a tube to push something along!

3. The Stomach 🌀

The food enters the stomach, a muscular bag. The stomach walls release gastric juice, which contains strong hydrochloric acid (HCl) and an enzyme called pepsin.
Action: The acid kills harmful bacteria, and pepsin begins the digestion of proteins. The stomach churns everything into a semi-liquid paste called 'chyme'.

4. The Small Intestine (Major Digestion & Absorption)

The chyme enters the small intestine. Here, it mixes with juices from three sources:
• Pancreas: Adds enzymes to digest carbohydrates, fats, and proteins.
• Liver: Produces bile, which helps break large fat globules into smaller ones (emulsification).
• Intestinal Wall: Secretes more enzymes to complete digestion. Now, the nutrients are ready to be absorbed into the blood through tiny finger-like projections called villi.

5. The Large Intestine

The leftover material, which is mostly undigested food and water, moves into the large intestine. The main job here is to absorb most of the remaining water back into the body.

6. Rectum and Anus (Elimination)

The solid waste, now called feces, is stored in the rectum and finally eliminated from the body through the anus.
The journey is complete!
